**Paper Explanation:**

This paper suggests that certain sleep disorders, particularly those rooted in psychological factors, might be understood through a quantum physics analogy called the "Quantum Zeno Effect." This effect, in this context, means the persistent state of being awake is like a quantum system being "observed" too frequently, preventing it from transitioning to deep sleep. The authors propose that the "Quantum Anti-Zeno Effect" could be a therapeutic approach, where introducing a "third state" (perhaps a specific intervention) helps to break this observational barrier and facilitate the transition to deep sleep. They also outline practical steps for implementing this therapeutic concept.
